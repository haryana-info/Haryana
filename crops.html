Major Rabi Crops in Haryana:

WHEAT

  • Haryana has the largest area under cultivation for this crop.
  • The state contributes 13% to India‚Äôs total production.
  • Haryana ranks 4th in production, following Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, and Uttar Pradesh.
  • Sirsa is the largest producer in the state.
  • Key contributing districts include Jind, Bhiwani, Hisar, Kaithal, and Fatehabad.

BARLEY

  • Barley-Producing Districts: Ambala, Faridabad, Karnal, Mahendragarh, Jind, Bhiwani, Sonipat, Rohtak, Sirsa, and Hisar.

CHICKPEA

  • Chickpea is predominantly grown in the western parts of Haryana, including Rohtak, Bhiwani, Sirsa, Hisar, and Mahendragarh.
  • Bhiwani stands as the largest producer of chickpea in the state.
  • Following Bhiwani, the districts of Mahendragarh, Hisar, and Sirsa also contribute significantly to chickpea production.
  • Chickpea is an essential crop in the region, thriving in the semi-arid climate of western Haryana.

MUSTARD

  • Major Mustard-Producing Districts: Bhiwani, Mahendragarh, Rewari, Hisar, Sirsa, and Jhajjar.
  • Top Producer: Mahendragarh stands as the largest producer of mustard in the state.

Kharif crops depend on the monsoon rains and require hot and humid weather to grow.

Major Kharif Crops in Haryana:

RICE

  • Rice is the second most important crop in Haryana.
  • It is primarily cultivated in the north-eastern and central parts of the state.
  • The crop requires a significant amount of water for cultivation.
  • Karnal is the largest producer of rice in Haryana.
  • Haryana is also the largest processor and exporter of Basmati rice in the country.
  • Key rice-producing districts include Kaithal, Kurukshetra, Ambala, Jind, Sonipat, and Panipat.

MAIZE

  • Maize is cultivated in districts like Ambala, Panchkula, Yamunanagar, Kurukshetra, and Rohtak.
  • This crop requires less water and adequate sunshine for optimal growth.

BAJRA

  • Bajra is primarily grown in Haryana, with Bhiwani being the largest producer, followed by Mahendragarh as the second largest.
  • Other significant producing districts include Sirsa, Hisar, Fatehabad, Jind, Rohtak, and Faridabad.
  • Bajra requires less water for cultivation and typically matures within 2-3 months.

JAWAR

  • Rohtak is the largest producer of jowar in Haryana.
  • Other major producing districts include Jhajjar, Nuh, Palwal, Sonipat, and Rewari.
  • However, the area under jowar cultivation has decreased over the years.

SUGARCANE

  • Sugarcane is an important commercial crop in Haryana, contributing 3% to India‚Äôs total production.
  • It is primarily cultivated in the Yamuna basin, with districts like Yamunanagar, Ambala, Karnal, Kurukshetra, Jind, Sonipat, and Rohtak leading in production.
  • Yamunanagar is the largest producer, followed by Karnal and Kurukshetra.
  • The crop is also used for making products like gur and khandsari.
  • It occupies the maximum area under commercial crops in the state.
