Women's Traditional Attire

Attire

Daman (Ghagra): A long, pleated skirt that reaches the ankles, often crafted from vibrant fabrics and adorned with intricate embroidery or patterns.Kurti (Shirt):A short tunic, typically made of cotton or silk, featuring simple designs or embroidery.Chunni (Odhni/Dupatta):A long, colorful scarf draped over the head and shoulders, symbolizing modesty and respect. It's often embellished with embroidery, mirror work, or lace detailing. ​Ornaments:Traditional jewelry complements the attire, including silver anklets, necklaces, bangles, nose rings, and earrings. The borla, a traditional headpiece, is commonly worn during festive occasions.

Odhana

The Gota embroidered scarf, also known as Odhani, is worn on the head by women.

Piliya

It is a yellow colored shawl with red embroidery on the edges. It is given by the parents on the birth of a child. Women wear it and perform Jalwa pujan or well worship.

Dhimachh

It is a silk shawl worn by the bride at the time of marriage.

Kangh

This is a red coloured shawl,inside it there are many types like vine, spindle embroidery, plant etc. It is embroidered

Men's Traditional Attire

Attire

Dhoti: A rectangular piece of unstitched cloth, approximately 4 yards in length, wrapped around the waist and legs, resembling trousers. In Haryana, the dhoti is typically white, symbolizing peace and status. Different castes may have distinct styles of draping the dhoti.Kurta:A loose-fitting shirt, often made of silk or cotton, extending just above or below the knees. The kurta can be plain white or adorned with simple embroidery.Pagri (Turban):A significant accessory symbolizing honor and respect, the pagri varies in style and color based on the occasion. While white is common for daily wear, brighter colors are chosen for festivals and weddings.​

Angochha

A long cloth draped around the shoulders, serving multiple purposes, including protection from the sun and as a towel.

Jutti

Traditional leather footwear, such as juttis or mojris, complements the attire.​

Khes

It is worn in early winter and is made of thick cotton.

Kambal

It is a thick woollen cloth worn during winters. Mostly elder people wear it during winters. It is also called shawl.
